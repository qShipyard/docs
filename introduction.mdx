narratorlog is a self-hosted, open-source engineering communication tool that reads your git commits, understands them in the context of your codebase, and delivers the right story to every audience — automatically, and always with human approval before anything is published. You run it on your own infrastructure. Your code, your data, your control.

## The problem it solves

Engineering teams ship code every day. But the story of what they built rarely reaches the people who need to hear it — and when it does, it arrives too late, in the wrong language, or not at all.

This creates a communication gap that costs teams in real, measurable ways:

- **Product managers** chase engineers every Friday asking "what shipped this week?" and get a wall of commit hashes or a vague summary that took 30 minutes to write.
- **Marketing teams** find out about new features weeks after launch — too late to prepare copy, write announcements, or capitalise on momentum.
- **Engineering managers** have no clear picture of shipping velocity, which areas of the codebase are moving, or whether the last sprint was mostly features or maintenance.
- **Executives** read raw changelogs they cannot interpret, or rely on secondhand summaries that lose important detail.
- **Engineers themselves** spend time writing summaries and release notes that duplicate effort already captured in their commits and pull requests.

The root cause is a translation problem. Engineering work is captured in the language of code — commits, diffs, pull requests, linked issues. The rest of the organisation operates in different languages: features, benefits, decisions, velocity signals. narratorlog is the translation layer that sits between them.

## Who it's for

narratorlog produces a different output for each audience from a single scan. Every output goes through a human approval step before delivery.

| Audience | What they get |
|---|---|
| **Developers** | A technical summary of what changed, what might break, and what to review |
| **Engineering Managers** | Plain-English shipping signal — velocity, areas of activity, blockers resolved |
| **Product Managers** | A feature-oriented breakdown of what moved and what is ready to demo |
| **Marketing** | Benefit-focused copy with no jargon, ready to use in announcements |
| **Executives** | A high-level narrative of what the team is building and shipping |

## How narratorlog is different

**Self-hosted, always.** narratorlog runs on your infrastructure. Nothing is sent anywhere you have not explicitly configured. There is no SaaS version, no phone-home, and no vendor dependency beyond your chosen AI provider.

**Bring your own AI.** narratorlog is AI-provider agnostic. Connect your own Anthropic, OpenAI, Ollama, Groq, or custom LLM endpoint. For teams that use Ollama, zero data leaves your machine — even in deep scan mode.

**A plugin system built for extension.** Sources, AI providers, and output destinations are all plugins. Writing an output plugin requires only TypeScript. The plugin interface is stable and versioned.

**Human approval before anything ships.** AI generates drafts. You review, edit, and approve them. Nothing reaches an audience without explicit sign-off from a human on your team.

## Current status

narratorlog is working end to end. The Go pipeline, background worker, web app, and Docker setup are all implemented.

- **Sources:** GitHub, GitLab, and Bitbucket — connect each with a Personal Access Token (no OAuth app needed)
- **AI providers:** Anthropic Claude, OpenAI GPT-4o, and Ollama (Groq coming soon)
- **Delivery:** Slack, Notion, Discord, Linear, and email
