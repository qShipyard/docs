narratorlog bridges the gap between your engineering team and everyone else. It reads what your team shipped, translates it for each audience — developers, product managers, marketing, and executives — then routes the drafts through a human approval step before delivering them to Slack, Notion, email, and more.

## What narratorlog does

Engineering teams ship code every day. But the story of what they built rarely reaches the people who need to hear it. narratorlog closes that gap automatically.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Audience-Specific Drafts" icon="users">
    Developers get technical summaries. Product gets plain-English breakdowns. Marketing gets benefit-focused copy. Executives get high-level narratives.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Human Approval First" icon="circle-check">
    Every draft goes through your team's review and approval before a single word is published anywhere.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Bring Your Own AI" icon="brain">
    Works with Anthropic Claude, OpenAI GPT-4o, or Ollama for fully local inference. Custom HTTP endpoints are also supported.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Your Data Stays Yours" icon="lock">
    narratorlog is self-hosted and open source. Your code, your data, your infrastructure — nothing leaves your environment unless you choose it.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Get started in three steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Run the quickstart">
    Start the full stack with a single Docker Compose command — no config required for local trials.
    ```shell
    docker compose -f deploy/docker-compose.quickstart.yml up -d --build
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete the setup wizard">
    Open [http://localhost:3000](http://localhost:3000), create your workspace and admin account, then connect your git provider and AI key in the activation flow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run your first scan">
    Connect a repository, trigger a manual scan, review the generated drafts for each audience, approve them, and watch narratorlog deliver them to your configured outputs.
  </Step>
</Steps>
